# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary — Mongolian apartment seekers (~90% on mobile):** People looking for a new home in Ulaanbaatar. They arrive emotionally — wanting to feel what it's like to live there before committing to a viewing. Their job: understand the space, find a unit that fits their life, and book with confidence. They make decisions on their phone, often sharing the site with a partner.

**Secondary — The developer client (B2B):** The real estate developer who licenses this template. They want a premium brand presence that stands apart from the cluttered Mongolian portal market, and a steady stream of qualified viewing leads. They judge the site against international high-end comparables.

## Product Purpose

A pre-sale apartment marketing website — a reusable, config-driven template re-skinned per developer client. The site exists to convert browsers into viewing appointments by making buyers feel the quality of the building before they ever step inside. Success means qualified leads, not traffic. The same engine extends to resort and vacation property sites.

## Brand Personality

Calm · Premium · Honest

## References

High-end property marketing like Extell and One57 — specifically: the use of negative space as a signal of quality, confident restraint over decorative excess, and copy that states rather than sells. Architecture leads; the site gets out of the way.

## Anti-references

- **Busy Mongolian property portals** — data-dense, low-contrast, cluttered listings that treat the buyer as a search query rather than a person
- **Western luxury clichés** — gold gradients, marble textures, serif-on-serif overload, the "expensive" visual language that signals excess rather than taste

## Design Principles

1. **Let the architecture speak** — every element earns its place. Restraint is the loudest signal of quality.
2. **Cinematic before informational** — the scroll-scrub tour is the emotional argument; facts (floor plans, specs, prices) follow feeling.
3. **One beautiful thing at a time** — high layout variance across sections, but single focal point per section. Never compete with yourself.
4. **Honest luxury** — warmth from material palette (oak, cream, sage), not from ornamentation. No decorative excess.
5. **Mobile-first, always** — ~90% of buyers arrive on a phone. The experience must feel native there, not adapted.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Visible keyboard focus on all interactive elements. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected (static fallback for all animated sections). Mongolian Cyrillic rendered correctly (Golos Text, `cyrillic-ext` subset). Alt text on all meaningful images.
